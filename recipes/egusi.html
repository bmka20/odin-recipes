Egusi Soup Recipe

Egusi soup is one of my favorite African dishes. My Nigerian side of my family makes this dish a lot. It's a warm soup made with a lot of vegetables. You can also put whatever meat you want inside of it. I usually prefer to put goat meat or beef in it. Some other popular choices are chicken and fish.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of blended onions
  • 4 cups of egusi (melon seeds)
  • 1/2 - 1 cup of palm oil
  • 2 teaspoons of fresh Une
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Crayfish (to taste)
  • 7 - 8 cups of stock
  • Crooked Meat (amount persona preference)
  • 2 cups of cut pumpkin leaves
  • 1 cup of cut waterleaf
  • 3 tablespoons of washed bitter leaf

Steps

  1. In large pot, heat palm oil on medium then add Une
  2. Slowly add stock then set to low to simmer
  3. Scoop teaspoon size balls (ball shape important) of egusi mix into stock
  4. Leave to simmer for 20-30 minutes so balls cook through
  5. Add the pumpkin leaves
  6. Add waterleaf
  7. Stir then put lid on pot for 7-10 minutes until the leaves wilt
  8. Add bitter leaf
  9. Take off lid and cook for another 5-10 minutes
  10. Taste and adjust seasoning accordingly
  11. Enjoy!
